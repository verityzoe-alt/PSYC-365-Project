Fine-Tuning the Details:
Post-encoding Music Differentially Impacts General and Detailed Memory

Kayla R. Clark and Stephanie L. Leal

I. PREVIOUS RESEARCH

  • 1) Music has been suggested to enhance memory consolidation by inducing emotional arousal, known as the emotional arousal hypothesis.

  • 2) Features that influence music-induced emotional arousal include emotional valence, familiarity, and pleasure derived from listening.

  • 3) In emotional memory, there is a tradeoff between gist-based and detailed information, in accordance with the Yerkes-Dodson law.

  • 4) In studying episodic memory, mnemonic discrimination tasks are often used to isolate detail-based pattern separation from gist-based pattern completion.

Big picture question:

How does post-encoding music influence general (gist) versus detailed memory?
